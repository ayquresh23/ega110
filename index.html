What is analytical chemistry?

Definition

Analytical chemistry is the science that develops and applies methods, instruments, and strategies to obtain information on the composition and nature of matter.

In practice: you have a sample вҖ” soil, water, a tablet, an alloy вҖ” and you want to know what's in it and how much. Every technique in this module is a different answer to that question.

Accuracy vs Precision

These two words are used interchangeably in everyday language. In analytical chemistry they mean strictly different things and you cannot use them interchangeably.

Accuracy

How close a measured value is to the true (accepted) value.

Measured by: error (absolute or relative).

A result can be precise but inaccurate вҖ” if a systematic error consistently shifts all measurements the same way.

Precision

How close replicate measurements are to one another.

Measured by: standard deviation.

A result can be precise but inaccurate, or accurate but imprecise (scattered around the true value).

рҹҺҜ
Think of a dartboard. Accurate = darts clustered around the bullseye. Precise = darts clustered tightly together (even if away from the bullseye). You want both вҖ” tight cluster on the bullseye.

Types of Error

Random (indeterminate) error

Unpredictable, affects precision. Caused by uncontrollable variables вҖ” air currents, vibrations, fluctuating power supply. Leads to scatter around the mean.

Cannot be eliminated вҖ” only reduced by taking more measurements and averaging.

Systematic (determinate) error

Consistent, reproducible, affects accuracy. Always shifts results in the same direction by roughly the same amount.

Can in principle be identified and corrected. Sources: instrumental (uncalibrated zero), methodological (wrong procedure), personal (observer bias), environmental.

Gross error

Occasional large mistakes вҖ” spilling sample, misreading a number, recording wrong units. Produces outliers. Detected by the Q-test (covered in Topic 2).

Four Types of Gravimetric Analysis

1. Precipitation

Analyte converted to insoluble precipitate, filtered, dried or ignited, weighed. Most common. Basis of this topic.

2. Volatilisation

Mass change from a volatile component (COвӮӮ, HвӮӮO) released by heating. Measured directly (mass loss) or by trapping in a weighed absorbent.

3. Electrogravimetry

Analyte electrodeposited onto a pre-weighed platinum electrode. Mass gain = mass of analyte. Very accurate вҖ” no coprecipitation.

4. Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA)

Mass recorded continuously as temperature increases at a controlled rate. Decomposition steps appear as discrete mass-loss events.

Coprecipitation Errors

Surface adsorption

Ions adsorb onto crystal surface. Minimised by washing with appropriate wash solution.

Occlusion

Impurities physically trapped inside crystal during rapid growth. Cannot be removed by washing. Minimised by slow precipitation and digestion.

Mechanical entrapment

Mother liquor trapped within precipitate mass. Minimised by proper filtration.

Post-precipitation

A second compound nucleates on the precipitate surface during prolonged standing. Minimised by filtering promptly.

MCQ Traps

Plausible-but-wrong claims the exam uses. Read each one. Know what's actually true.

Topics 1 & 2 вҖ” Statistics

Stats T1
"Standard deviation measures accuracy."
FALSE. Standard deviation measures precision. Accuracy is measured by error (how close to the true value). You can have high precision with poor accuracy.
Stats T2
"The z-based confidence interval is wider than the t-based CI at the same confidence level."
FALSE. The t-based CI is always wider. t accounts for additional uncertainty from estimating Пғ from a small sample. t-values are always вүҘ z (equal only at df = вҲһ).
Stats T3
"In the Q-test, the gap is the distance from the suspect value to the next-nearest neighbour."
FALSE. The gap is |x_q вҲ’ nearest neighbour|. Always sort the data first, then take the difference to the adjacent value.
Stats T4
"After identifying an outlier with the Q-test, you can include it in the recalculated mean."
FALSE. After rejecting an outlier, recalculate mean and SD on the remaining NвҲ’1 data points only.
Stats T5
"The sample standard deviation formula divides by N."
FALSE. Sample SD divides by NвҲ’1 (Bessel's correction) to give an unbiased estimate of Пғ. Only the population SD divides by N.

Topic 3 вҖ” Gravimetric

Grav T1
"1 mole of FeSвӮӮ produces 1 mole of BaSOвӮ„."
FALSE. FeSвӮӮ contains 2 sulfur atoms, each forming one SOвӮ„ВІвҒ» вҶ’ one BaSOвӮ„. So 1 mol FeSвӮӮ вҶ’ 2 mol BaSOвӮ„. Forgetting this stoichiometry halves your answer.
Grav T2
"Occlusion can be removed by washing the precipitate."
FALSE. Occluded impurities are physically trapped inside the crystal lattice вҖ” washing cannot reach them. Only digestion (slow crystal growth, Ostwald ripening) reduces occlusion.
Grav T3
"TGA mass loss between 600вҖ“800В°C represents the mass of CaO lost."
FALSE. The mass lost is COвӮӮ (from CaCOвӮғ вҶ’ CaO + COвӮӮ). CaO remains in the crucible. Use m(COвӮӮ) to find n(COвӮӮ) = n(CaCOвӮғ), then calculate m(CaCOвӮғ).

"Electrogravimetry requires a gravimetric factor calculation."
FALSE. In electrogravimetry, the analyte is deposited as the pure element on the electrode вҖ” mass gain directly equals mass of analyte. No GF needed.

Topic 4 вҖ” Spectroscopy

Spec T1
"When light enters a denser medium, its frequency decreases."
FALSE. Frequency (and energy) are unchanged when light changes media. Only velocity and wavelength change (both decrease in a denser medium).
Spec T2
"Measuring absorbance without a blank is fine because the blank has zero absorbance."
FALSE. Solvents, cuvettes, and dissolved species all absorb. Without blank correction you overestimate the analyte absorbance. Always measure I_blank.
Spec T3
"In standard addition: C_analyte = A_analyte / A_spiked Г— C_spike."

Spec T4
"Glass cuvettes can be used for UV measurements."
FALSE. Glass absorbs UV radiation. Use quartz (SiOвӮӮ) cuvettes for UV. Glass and plastic are only suitable for Vis (400вҖ“800 nm).

Topic 5 вҖ” Titrations

Tit T1
"The equivalence point for a weak acid/strong base titration is at pH 7."
FALSE. The equivalence point is basic (pH > 7) because the product is the conjugate base AвҒ», which is weakly basic. Choose an indicator that changes above pH 7 (e.g. phenolphthalein).
Tit T2
"EDTA reacts with CaВІвҒә in a 2:1 molar ratio."
FALSE. EDTA always reacts 1:1 with any metal ion regardless of its charge. CaВІвҒә, FeВівҒә, and ThвҒҙвҒә all consume exactly 1 mole of EDTA per mole of metal.
Tit T3
"In the Volhard method, you add KSCN directly to the original sample without filtering."
FALSE. You must filter off the AgX precipitate first. If AgCl remains, SCNвҒ» reacts with it at the surface (Ksp AgSCN < Ksp AgCl) giving a falsely high AgвҒә value and wrong ClвҒ» result.
Tit T4
"The molar ratio of MnOвӮ„вҒ» to FeВІвҒә in permanganate titration is 1:1."
FALSE. MnOвӮ„вҒ» accepts 5 electrons (MnвҒ·вҒә вҶ’ MnВІвҒә); FeВІвҒә donates 1 electron. Ratio is 1 MnOвӮ„вҒ» : 5 FeВІвҒә. Forgetting this gives a result that is 5Г— too low.
Tit T5
"KMnOвӮ„ titrations require an external indicator."
FALSE. KMnOвӮ„ is self-indicating. The purple MnOвӮ„вҒ» is immediately decolourised by FeВІвҒә before the endpoint. The first permanent purple colour marks the endpoint. No separate indicator is needed.
Tit T6
"Eriochrome Black T indicator can be used at pH 5."
FALSE. At pH < 6.3, free EBT is in the red HвӮӮEBTвҒ» form вҖ” the same colour as the metal-EBT complex. You cannot see the endpoint. EBT must be used at pH > 6.3 (typically pH 10).
Tit T7
"The half-equivalence point pH equals the pKb for a weak acid titration."
FALSE. At the half-equivalence point of a weak acid titration, pH = pKa. The pKb relates to the conjugate base. Don't confuse which ionisation constant applies.
